Black Rhino

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Diceros bicornis
  • Population: Less than 5,500
  • Habitat: Various African savannas
  • Threats: Poaching, habitat loss

Mountain Gorilla

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Gorilla beringei beringei
  • Population: Around 1,000
  • Habitat: Albertine Rift montane forests in Uganda, Rwanda, DRC
  • Threats: Habitat loss, poaching, disease, civil unrest

African Forest Elephant

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta cyclotis
  • Population: 40,000-50,000
  • Habitat: Dense rainforests of Central and West Africa
  • Threats: Poaching for ivory, habitat loss, human conflict

Ethiopian Wolf

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Canis simensis
  • Population: Less than 500
  • Habitat: Ethiopian highlands above 3,000m
  • Threats: Habitat loss, disease, hybridization with domestic dogs

African Wild Dog

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Lycaon pictus
  • Population: 6,600 adults
  • Habitat: Grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa
  • Threats: Habitat fragmentation, human conflict, disease

Grevy's Zebra

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Equus grevyi
  • Population: About 2,000
  • Habitat: Semi-arid grasslands of Kenya and Ethiopia
  • Threats: Habitat loss, competition with livestock, hunting

Addax

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Addax nasomaculatus
  • Population: Less than 100 in wild
  • Habitat: Sahara desert region of Niger and Chad
  • Threats: Hunting, oil exploration, political instability

Cheetah

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus
  • Population: Less than 7,000
  • Habitat: Grasslands and open plains of sub-Saharan Africa
  • Threats: Habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, prey depletion

African Penguin

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Spheniscus demersus
  • Population: Around 21,000 pairs
  • Habitat: Coastal areas of South Africa and Namibia
  • Threats: Commercial fishing, oil spills, climate change
