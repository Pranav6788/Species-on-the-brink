Black Rhino

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Gymnogyps californianus
  • Population: Less than 30
  • Habitat: Ranged across much of North America, from Canada down to Mexico
  • Threats: Lead Poisoning, habitat loss, Poaching and Human Disturbance, Climate Change, Invasive Species and Predation

Whooping Crane

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Grus americana
  • Population: About 600 individuals
  • Habitat: Wetlands, marshes, and shallow coastal areas across central North America, primarily in Alberta, Canada, and Texas, USA.
  • Threats: Habitat destruction, Climate change, Human disturbances

Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Lepidochelys kempii
  • Population: Around 10,000 nesting females (slowly increasing).
  • Habitat: Coastal areas and beaches along the Gulf of Mexico and southern Texas.
  • Threats: Bycatch, Habitat destruction, Pollution, Climate change

Jaguars

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Panthera onca
  • Population: Estimated 50-100 in the wild in the southwestern U.S. (Arizona, New Mexico).
  • Habitat: Dense forests, tropical rainforests, and riverine areas in the Southern U.S., Mexico, and Central America.
  • Threats: Poaching, Loss of habitat, Human-wildlife conflict, Climate change

Gray Wolf

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus
  • Population: Estimated 6,000 individuals in the wild.
  • Habitat: Northern Rockies, Great Lakes region, Alaska, and parts of Canada.
  • Threats: Poaching and hunting, Habitat fragmentation, Human-wildlife conflict

Saimaa Seal

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Phoca hispida saimensis
  • Population: Less than 400 individuals.
  • Habitat: Freshwater lakes of the Saimaa region in Finland.
  • Threats: Pollution in the lakes, Climate change, Bycatch, Habitat loss
