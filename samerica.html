Amazon River Dolphin

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Inia geoffrensis
  • Population: Decreasing, exact numbers unknown
  • Habitat: Rivers and lakes in the Amazon and Orinoco river basins
  • Threats: Dam construction, fishing bycatch, pollution, boat traffic

Golden Lion Tamarin

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Leontopithecus rosalia
  • Population: About 2,500
  • Habitat: Atlantic coastal forests of Brazil
  • Threats: Deforestation, habitat fragmentation, wildlife trade

Andean Bear

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Tremarctos ornatus
  • Population: Less than 18,000
  • Habitat: Andean mountains from Venezuela to Bolivia
  • Threats: Habitat loss, hunting, human-wildlife conflict

Blue-throated Macaw

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Ara glaucogularis
  • Population: Less than 300 in wild
  • Habitat: Northern Bolivia's savanna and palm swamps
  • Threats: Habitat loss, illegal pet trade, competition for nest sites

Giant Otter

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Pteronura brasiliensis
  • Population: 1,000-5,000 individuals
  • Habitat: Amazon, Orinoco, and La Plata river systems
  • Threats: Mining pollution, habitat destruction, hunting

Darwin's Fox

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Lycalopex fulvipes
  • Population: Fewer than 1,000 adults
  • Habitat: Temperate rainforests of Chile
  • Threats: Habitat loss, dogs (disease and predation), road kills
